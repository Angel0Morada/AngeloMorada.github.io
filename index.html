Course Description

The course offers a comprehensive exploration of traditional predictive modeling techniques and their critical role in shaping modern business strategies. It delves into classical statistical and machine learning approaches, focusing on their application in uncovering actionable insights from data. Students will gain a thorough understanding of core predictive modeling algorithms, including linear and logistic regression, decision trees, k-nearest neighbors, and ensemble methods. In addition to theoretical foundations, the course emphasizes practical applications through hands-on exercises and real-world case studies. Students will develop proficiency in model evaluation, hyperparameter tuning, and the interpretation of predictive outcomes, equipping them to strategically address complex business challenges.

To further enhance learning, the course introduces fundamental concepts in neural networks using multi-layer perceptrons (MLPs). This introduction prepares students for more advanced topics in neural networks and deep learning, which will be covered in subsequent courses. Coursework includes regular assignments and a final project, all utilizing the Python programming language to ensure students acquire both theoretical knowledge and practical programming skills.

Activities

Prelims

For the preliminary term we were tasked on understanding how the models work and the process of building different models the first model that was taught to us is k-Nearest Neighbor or also known KNN which is one of the simplest models and easy to understand and we developed this model in the Goggle Colab IDE. After understanding how to use Google Colab we were tasked with building another model Simple and Multiple linear regression both of this activity is required to create a report in IEEE format then after submission we also tasked to create a peer review to our other classmateâ€™s work in order to further understand how the models work.

My key take away from the Preliminary activities, quizzes, and exam is that to understand advance topics in Machine Learning we must first understand the basic to develop and implement such models if we want to use and implement advanced version of machine learning models.

Midterms

For the midterm we are now tasked with developing both Logistic Regression and Decision, after developing both of the models we are tasked with comparing the two models which is the best model between the two after understanding we gathered what we understand and record our findings in a research paper in a IEEE format. after that we were asked to be grouped into three people for a project and create a research proposal about a specific industry and develop a model that can be used in that industry but before developing we were only asked to create a paper and a presentation on what industry we selected.

My key take away from the Midterm is that the two models that we have studied and developed has its own strengths and weaknesses there are models that can run in a certain dataset but cannot perform well on another dataset so my understanding is that models can be used on anything but result may vary depending on what dataset we used it on. For the group project we are picked the real estate industry and develop a model that can predict prices based on the features of an apartment.

Finals

For our Final Term we were taught how to develop Ensemble Learning that consist multiple models that will run layer by layer to create a polished output. Then we learned the basic of Neural Network and developed MLP model or Multi-Layer Perceptron after that we then proceed to develop our project which the basic function of the model is to predict rental pricing base on features that renters sought after. This activities and project has and individual research paper in an IEEE format.

My key takeaway from this last term is that Machine learning is integral to different industries and Implementing them has efficiently helped each industry in a certain way this subject showed me that every industry including the IT industry converge to Machine Learning and AI in the future, the models that we created are the stepping stone in our understanding of Machine Learning and AI.
